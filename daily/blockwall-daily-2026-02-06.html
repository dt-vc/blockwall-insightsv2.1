U.S. Marshals Service under scrutiny after insider allegedly stole $25 million from federal crypto wallets

Jeff John Roberts • Security • Score: 7/10

The U.S. Marshals Service is investigating an alleged insider theft of approximately $25 million in cryptocurrency from government-controlled wallets, with the suspect identified as John Daghita, son of the CEO of Command Services & Support (CMDSS), a contractor hired to manage seized digital assets. The stolen funds originated from wallets holding cryptocurrency seized in connection with the 2016 Bitfinex hack, and were traced through multiple exchanges and bridges in a structured laundering operation before roughly $700,000 remained unaccounted for. CMDSS won a $40 million contract in October 2024 to manage confiscated cryptocurrencies despite lacking SEC and FINRA licenses, exposing vulnerabilities in how the federal government secures billions of dollars in seized crypto assets. This incident underscores systemic risks in government crypto custody, as the Marshals Service has historically relied on basic spreadsheet tracking and lacks comprehensive internal controls for managing its digital asset holdings.
