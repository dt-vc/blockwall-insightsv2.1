$2.9B Bitcoin ETF Outflow, Bearish Futures Data Project More BTC Downside

News • Bitcoin • Score: 7/10

U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs have recorded over $2.9 billion in net outflows across the past 12 trading days, averaging $243 million daily since January 16 and signaling institutional de-risking amid waning demand. This capital drain coincides with Bitcoin's 26% price drop over three weeks to around $70,000, its lowest since November 2024, confirming a bearish inverse cup-and-handle pattern on the daily chart that projects further downside toward $65,000 or $60,000. The outflows, coupled with $3.25 billion in leveraged long liquidations purging positions above 4x leverage, heighten downside risks for institutional portfolios as ETF flows must stabilize to counter sustained selling pressure. Rising exchange reserves add to supply overhang, underscoring the need for investors to monitor liquidity metrics closely.
